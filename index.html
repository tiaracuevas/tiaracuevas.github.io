About Me

  • Psychology

    State University of New York at Geneseo

    I've always wanted to understand how humans think. So, in college I studied psychology (BA) with a minor in human development. I became fascinated with cognitive psychology.

  • School Psychology

    University at Buffalo

    After college, I wanted to understand how people learn. I went on to get my masters degree in School Psychology. Aside from assessing IQ and academic achievement, I had opportunities to collect/interpret data, collaborate with teams, and develop student-focused solutions during my career as a school psychologist.

  • Charlotte Photo Booth Fun

    Charlotte, NC

    After graduate school, I married my wonderful husband who owns a photo booth rental company. It was a new venture for us, so I had the chance to try out lots of different roles within the company. Ultimately, I found my niche researching current photo booth technology, keeping up with market trends, maintaining our website, and working with individual customers to design their custom photo booth experience. I fell in love with the opportunities to be creative and innovative.

  • Coding

    UNC Charlotte

    So I decided I wanted to be creative, but how?! I spent some time researching potential interests and stumbled upon coding. It seemed interesting, but I had minimal coding experience. I took a leap of faith and enrolled in a Coding Boot Camp at UNC Charlotte. It was such a rewarding experience and I now feel proficient with both front-end and back-end web development.

  • UX/UI Design

    Interaction Design Foundation

    In my free time, I began reading Norman's The Design of Everyday Things. Up until this point, I was trying to find a way to consolidate all of my skills/interests into a career. Once I realized that interaction design is the intersection of psychology and technology, it all clicked for me! I began studying UX/UI design independently and I completely fell in love with it.
